I'm an undergraduate at the **California Institute of Technology** studying Electrical Engineering (Computer Engineering track) with a minor in Robotics. My research interests center on **robot perception, multimodal sensing, and autonomous systems** â€” I want to build robots that keep working when the world refuses to cooperate.

I'm currently a **Research Assistant in the Autonomous Robotics and Control Lab (ARCL) at Caltech**, working with **Professor Soon-Jo Chung** on thermal-image-based autonomous navigation for aerial robots in low-light, over-water environments using imitation learning from traditional planners. I was named a **Caltech Class of '52 SURF Fellow** for this work.

Previously, I worked with **Professor Zhonghua Xu** at the **University of Texas at Arlington** on space weather research. I developed **Global-GMDs**, a visualization tool for mapping geomagnetic disturbances, published in **Elsevier SoftwareX**, and I wrote the **SuperMAG R API** used by 4,000+ registered users worldwide. I also built a soft-body robotic snake recognized with a Bronze Medal at the 2023 Genius Olympiad.

I reached the **USACO Platinum Division** and hold an **Amateur Radio Technician Class license (KK7NWF)**. My long-term goal is to pursue a **PhD in Robotics**.
