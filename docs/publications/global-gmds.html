Global-GMDs: The Global Map of Geomagnetic Disturbances

Elsevier SoftwareX 2023
Hongyi Hu (First & Corresponding Author), Zhonghua Xu
Journal Space Weather

Abstract

To improve the understanding and monitoring the impacts of geomagnetic disturbances (GMDs) on power grids globally, the presented software, Global-GMDs, uses magnetic field measurements from geomagnetic observatories worldwide and the Kriging method to generate global maps of GMDs. It provides better observational information during a solar storm to power grid operations and other crucial infrastructures. It can also help researchers to assess the GMDs prediction model by comparing with Global-GMDs maps and to get better understanding of physics mechanisms.

Key Highlights

  • Published in Elsevier SoftwareX as first and corresponding author.
  • Uses data from over 200 geomagnetic observatories during the 2003 Halloween Storm, the strongest solar storm in the last three decades.
  • Employs Kriging geostatistical interpolation to generate continuous global maps of GMDs at 0.5° spatial resolution and 1-minute temporal resolution.
  • Validated through cross-validation: 60.1% of stations achieved ≤15% relative MAE, 31.1% between 15–45%, and only 8.8% above 45%.
  • Reveals that higher-density observatory networks in high-latitude regions (Europe, Canada) significantly improve interpolation accuracy where disturbances are most intense.
  • Global maps trace storm inception in the auroral zone, propagation to mid-latitudes, peak intensity (>2800 nT), and recovery phases.

Tech Stack

  • MATLAB & Mapping Toolbox — Data processing, Kriging interpolation, and global map visualization.
  • Python 3, PIL, glob — GIF synthesis and post-processing.
  • SuperMAG — Ground magnetometer data source (~200 stations).
  • NASA ACE Satellite — Solar wind and IMF data.
